New mothers' circle

Becoming a mother brings a lot of challenges. Becoming a mother away from friends and family, in another country brings even more challenges. We know, we’ve been there.

During the first several months - which are often called the “4th trimester” - you and your partner have to face many changes. While it can be an opportunity for a fresh start, old patterns and roles, various expectations and memories can kick in as well.

Sharing the joys and the challenges with other new moms, in a safe and intimate atmosphere with the help of a professional, can make a huge difference in your sense of well-being. It will help you reach a deeper understanding of yourself and your relationship to your baby, helping you to become the mother you always wanted to be. Mamakör is a community where all this can begin.

The workshop

Mamakör - Mother’s Circle is a series of 6 weekly, two-hour-long meetings that take place in a closed (i.e., not drop in) group setting for mothers and their newborns (0-5 months).

Each session has a particular focus to help you understand the joys and challenges of this vulnerable time in a mother’s life. Some of the areas we are going to discuss are: your birthing experience, changes that your relationship might have to deal with, the meaning of being a mother, and fighting and/or embracing all the expectations that are poured onto a new mom, etc.

The meetings are facilitated by experienced therapists who are mothers themselves and/or became mothers in another country, far away from home. The group is inspired by the personal experience of a Hungarian mother in San Francisco, and has been running in Hungary since her return last year to serve new mothers and their families.
